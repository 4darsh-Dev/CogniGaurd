Know About Dark Patterns

Understand the different types of dark patterns and how to recognize them.

Types of Dark Patterns

Bait and Switch

Users think they are doing one thing, but something else happens instead.

Example: Clicking a button to close a popup but it opens a new page instead.

Privacy Zuckering

Tricking users into publicly sharing more information than they intended.

Example: Automatically making user profiles public without explicit consent.

Roach Motel

Easy to get into a situation, but hard to get out of it (e.g., subscription).

Example: Subscribing to a service easily but having to call customer service to cancel.

Sneak into Basket

Adding additional products to your cart without your consent.

Example: Adding insurance to a product purchase without asking.

Confirmshaming

Guilt-tripping users into opting into something.

Example: "No, I don't want to save money" as an option to decline an offer.

Forced Continuity

Making it difficult to cancel a service or subscription.

Example: Free trials that automatically convert to paid subscriptions without reminder.
